Greatest Hotels

Web Design, Branding

Greatest Hotels is a high class presentation of hotel resorts with Google business photos to drive traffic towards the greatest hotels in the world.

Concept Objectives

The objective of the project was designing a visual identity and website for the client, Greatest Hotels Group (GHG). The goal of the website was to curate the best hotels in the world and encourage users to explore them with Google Business Photos and promote direct reservations.

Users can learn all about the hotels they like, add them to lists, and become informed about their rooms and features. This project included user testing, user feedback, and personas to create a reliable tool for business or pleasure.

User Research

GHG is a collection of the greatest hotels in the world. By walking through luxury 4 and 5 star resorts, Greatest Hotels is focused on showcasing quality over value. Greatest Hotels’ target audience are upscale consumers looking to travel at 4 and 5 star resorts around the world. User Personas and design decisions were made with middle-aged americans in mind.

  • Armando Berlitz, 43, Male
  • German American, Born & Resident of America

  • Expertise: Industrial-Organizational Psychologist

Extroverted and critical. If the site does not look like it was made to find the hotel he wants or looks untrustworthy enough, he would easily leave to use the sites he is used to. Armando keeps his social networks to family & friends, and keeps his online behavior professional.

Branding

Greatest Hotels’ old brand was for a different goal. The visual language didn’t age well and was from a time when Flash was a heavy hitter in web technology. As the client expressed their desire for change I worked with them closely. The style guide was redefined through various iterations for a modern and sophisticated look fitting for their target audience. Each round of options were shaped through feedback into the simplistic and sturdy style used for the rest of the visuals.

Wireframing

The wireframes for this project were fairly low in definition as most of the space is dedicated to the photography and interactive Google Business Photos area. Details are more spent on the organization and placement of content

and the features that interact with it. Components like location listings were wireframed to be consistent in functionality when used in different pages. I made around 10 wireframes for this project with two versions.

Screenshots

I started working on final compositions after user testing the wireframes and making a UI style guide. I made this project’s compositions in Photoshop but this was one of the last times I favored Photoshop before switching to Sketch.

If the client or team requires Photoshop, I’ll work with it. While I think Sketch is the better tool for the job, being cooperative is much more important. If there is no real reason for using Photoshop for a project, I’ll ask if I can use what tools work best for me.
